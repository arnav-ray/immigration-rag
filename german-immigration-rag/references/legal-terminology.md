# German Immigration Law — Authoritative Terminology Mapping

Master reference for English ↔ German term equivalence.
Use these mappings when translating queries before retrieval.
Do NOT paraphrase German legal terms — use the exact terms below.

---

## Residence Permits (Aufenthaltstitel)

| English Term | German Term | § Reference | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Temporary residence permit | Aufenthaltserlaubnis | § 7 AufenthG | General category; many sub-types |
| Settlement permit / Permanent residence | Niederlassungserlaubnis | § 9 AufenthG | Third-country nationals only |
| EU long-term residence permit | Erlaubnis zum Daueraufenthalt-EU | § 9a AufenthG | Third-country nationals only — NOT for EU citizens |
| EU Blue Card | Blaue Karte EU | § 18g AufenthG | Replaces old § 19a (2023 reform) |
| Opportunity Card | Chancenkarte | § 20a AufenthG | Points-based job-search permit |
| ICT Card (intracompany transfer) | ICT-Karte | § 19 AufenthG | |
| Mobile ICT Card | Mobiler-ICT-Karte | § 19b AufenthG | |
| Visa | Visum | § 6 AufenthG | |
| National visa (D-Visa) | Nationales Visum | § 6 Abs. 3 AufenthG | Long-stay visa (90+ days) |
| EU permanent residence (EU citizen) | Recht auf Daueraufenthalt | § 4a FreizügG/EU | EU citizens after 5 years — NOT § 9a |

> **Critical distinctions:**
> - § 4 AufenthG defines the exhaustive formal list of residence titles for third-country nationals
> - § 28 is NOT a standalone title type — it is a sub-category of Aufenthaltserlaubnis for family members of German citizens
> - § 19a (old Blue Card) was replaced by § 18g in the 2023 Fachkräfteeinwanderungsgesetz reform — do not cite § 19a as current

## Legal Regime Separation Rules

Always apply this table before answering. Never mix regimes in one answer
without explicitly labelling them.

| User type | Governing law | Key rule |
|---|---|---|
| EU/EEA citizen | FreizügG/EU | Rights arise automatically from EU law. AufenthG conditions do NOT apply. |
| Non-EU national (third-country) | AufenthG | Visa, residence permit, language test, financial proof requirements apply. |
| Employment permit approval | BeschV | Governs which occupations require Federal Employment Agency consent. |
| Non-EU spouse of EU citizen | FreizügG/EU § 3 | Direct family member. Automatic derived right. No prior cohabitation required. |
| Other close persons (non-family, EU context) | FreizügG/EU § 3a | Discretionary — not automatic. Do not apply § 3a conditions to spouses. |
| Non-EU spouse of German citizen | AufenthG § 28 Abs. 1 Nr. 1 | For initial issuance. § 31 governs post-divorce independent residence only. |
| German citizen | N/A | Not subject to German immigration law. AufenthG / FreizügG / BeschV do not apply. |
| Non-EU national seeking permanent residence | AufenthG § 9 (Niederlassungserlaubnis) | After generally 5 years; conditions include income, language, contributions. |
| EU citizen seeking permanent residence | FreizügG/EU § 4a | After 5 years of lawful residence. Separate from § 9 AufenthG. |
